Surpassing Expectations - Paris Eternal

The Paris Eternal (5-2) have given the entire Overwatch League a shock with their performance through the first two months of play. After their lackluster 2019 campaign resulting in an 11-17 record, the Eternal retooled their roster and infused some Korean talent into their previously all-European lineup. Of course the standout this season is Xzi, whose McCree has been easily one of the best in the league. He has sizeable leads in final blows per 10 minutes (12.1) and solo kills per 10 (2.8), as well as being behind only KSP (LAV) in eliminations per 10 (21.5) and damage per 10 (10,146) as the cowboy. However, it'd be a disservice to the rest of the Eternal to not mention their fantastic play. Hanbin’s D.Va has been fantastic, with high numbers in damage per 10 and final blows per 10 (6,738.9 and 7.4 respectively), combined with having great positioning to frequently negate enemy Blizzards and shield attacks. FDGod’s Lúcio has been one of - if not THE most - aggressive Lúcio in the league, leading all Lúcios in final blows per 10 (4.0) and being beaten only by Masaa (ATL) in solo kills per 10 (0.6). The best part is that all of this comes before spark1e, one of the hottest free agents of the offseason, has even hit the OWL age requirement, which means they can still go up from here. Their team play is so consistent and aggressive that they won against Carpe and the Philadelphia Fusion - arguably the strongest team in the league so far - in a greuling match that went a full five maps in week 5. The Biggest Letdown - Toronto Defiant

After a dismal 2019 season (with an 8-20 record), the Toronto Defiant (3-4) completely blew up their roster with RoKy, Logix and Mangachu being the only remaining players from last season. The Defiant then went on a free agent shopping spree, picking up players from the Los Angeles Valiant (Agilities, KariV), Los Angeles Gladiators (Surefour), Boston Uprising (Kellex), San Francisco Shock (Nevix) and Fusion University (Beast). With KariV earning a 2019 Role Star award and a team of strong North American and European players being added, many fans and analysts believed that Toronto would be a strong mid-field contender, with a worst-case scenario being a berth in the postseason play-in bracket. However, after a promising Opening Day win against Paris and a strong five-map showing against Philadelphia, the Defiant promptly fell flat against Atlanta, Houston and Florida. Their dive comp featuring Surefour’s Sombra and Agilities’ Doomfist was promptly figured out by other teams, and Beast’s Reinhardt play has been inconsistent at best after a strong first impression vs Paris. The situation seems bleak, and it seems like the players are playing as individuals rather than a whole team.
